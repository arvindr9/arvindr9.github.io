About me

Hello! My name is Arvind, and I am a sophomore at Georgia Tech. I am currently majoring in computer science. See my resume here.

Interests

I have a passion in theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence and am currently doing AI research. Aside from that, I have an interest in mathematics (especially in the field of combinatorics) and especially enjoy participating in math contests like ARML.

Skills

  • Machine Learning: I completed the Machine Learning course by the Stanford professor Andrew Ng. I now have knowledge of supervised learning algorithms and unsupervised learning algorithms. I also took his deep learning specialization in Python, which gave me skills of training deep neural networks and solving problems in image processing.

    I am currently doing research in the Interactive Optimization and Learning Lab under the supervision of Professor Sebastian Pokutta. I am currently focusing on fooling random forests classifiers with adversarial examples (more specifically in this case image samples that are perturbed by random but imperceptible noise) to refute former beliefs that random forests are robust to adversarial examples. I am also proposing a method to make random forests more robust to these adversarial examples. This website has many nice links to papers about adversarial examples and other areas of machine learning (like Online Learning and Reinforcement Learning)

  • Competitive programming:

    I have competed in contests like ICPC (International Collegiate Programming Contest) and enjoy solving ad-hoc problems that I haven't seen before.

    I generally use C++ for programming contests since its standard library has useful builtin data structures (pairs, sets, maps, queues, dequeues, etc...). I sometimes use Python for simpler problems that just require a few lines of code.

    I am currently practicing competitive programming under the former ICPC World Finalist Mostafa Saad. I feel that I am learning and improving a lot, and I hope to be among the top ten in the nation in my sophomore year.

  • Mathematics: I have participated in math competitions like ARML (American Regions Mathematics League) in high school, and as a president of my school's math team, I have been very involved in math. I am particularly interested by fields of discrete mathematics like combinatorics and number theory.

    I am currently the workshops coordinator of Georgia Tech's Big-O Theory Club, which involves running problem sessions. I have a tendency to pick challenging combinatorial or algorithmic problems (I recommend trying ISL 1994 C3; it's a fun problem). In this position, I have also run large workshops related to Machine Learning in areas such as Computer Vision and Tensorflow to over 150 people in total.


  • Violin: I have played the violin since 4th grade (in 2008) and started private lessons in 8th grade (2012). I played in the Georgia All-State Orchestra in 2015, 2016 and 2017 and was a member of the nationally recognized Atlanta Symphony Youth Orchestra in the 2016-2017 season. I have been playing in the Georgia Tech Symphony Orchestra (GTSO) since Fall 2017.
  • Conducting I am currently learning conducting under the GTSO conductor Chaowen Ting and have been learning it since September 2018. I have experience conducting Carmen Suite No. 1 (Bizet) and am currently focusing on the Overture movement of La forza del destino (Verdi). I have practiced conducting Georgia Tech's Chamber Orchestra.
